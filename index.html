Research Projects

Data Mining on weekly public health data of Dehradun district

Worked on weekly incidence data of 16 diseases for more than 100 regions in Dehradun. Obtained clusters of regions based on similarity patterns which were subsequently visualized using maps. Also, worked in predicting outbreaks of the diseases and specific regions contibuting maximum to the same.
Work submitted to Journal of Indian Society of Remote Sensing, Springer.

Empirical Study on Health care datasets

The work was aimed to perform a comparitive evaluation of classification algorithms on Health Care Datasets. Models were built for 13 datasets using Support Vector Machines, Naive Bayes, Classification Trees, Random Forests and Gradient Boosting. Performance was evaluted using Accuracy, Precision, Sensitivity and F1 Score. p-tests were used to study statistical significance.

Data Mining Techniques in Indian Health Care: a short review

Studied 61 research papers to analyse data science research in Health Care with a special focus on India. Highlighted the patient life cycle and identified the role of major entities at various stages of the lifecycle. Identified areas in Indian health system where data science can play a major role.

Application of Twitter in Health Care sector of India

Extracted tweets to form word clouds and perform top word analysis in order to detect disease patterns based on social media discussion. Emphasis was on studying the impact of Mission Indradhanush (Govt. of India initiative) aiming for full immunization of children.

Filter based Feature Selection using Hill Climbing approach

Studied Hill Climbing(HC) as a heuristic search technique. Variations of HC based on randomness, size of neigborhood and direction of search were also studied. Achieved 1.7% better classification accuracy compared to Genetic Algorithm on 20 datasets. Work submitted to Sadhana, Springer.

Feature Cluster Taxonomy and Ranking based Feature Selection

Developing feature clusters based on correlation among them. Obtaining best features from each cluster using Best First Search(BFS). Information Gain(IG) is used thereon to find the best features from the non-clustered features. Combining the BFS and IG results to obtain optimal feature subset. Work is ongoing.
